Why are houses so expensive!

05/03/2021

Hello everyone (or anyone), so I like to look forward and one of things I do to pass the time is look at houses for sale, I know it sounds sad but when theres nothing else to do, thats my go to! So currently Im in Exeter, and one thing I will say is the house prices here are outrageous! There are 1 bed tiny flats being sold for like £100,000 - £120,000? Like how can they justify those prices? Or even what drives these prices? For that amount of money you can buy a standard 2 / 3 bed terraced house from where I'm from in Wales! Now, I know my little town hasn't got he oppertunies for high paying jobs, and so maybe thats where you see that properties are purchased at higher prices, but for young people I look at it and just feel a little angry. The best opppertunies for young newly-graduated students are mostly in larger cities, but then because of that the demand is higher and the prices and deposits surge and so these young people endup rented and in the long run paying more than if they got a mortgage for a property. So something possible positive from this pandemic that may come out of it is the ability for more people to work from home, and it not become such a luxery. Obviously everyone is different and some people may prefer working in an office, but imagine getting a job with a company say in London, and they say you can work from home, with the power of the internet you could like in say Manchester, Nottingham or even South Wales and earn a competitive London Salary. And in these regions the prices of rent / houses are far less and I dont know about you, but I would much rather work from a nice 3 bedroon house where I could have my own office, then work in busy company office and live in a small cramped studio apartment in London! Even though i'm still new to the PhD, when I graduate I defineitly going to look for jobs where you can work from home! Hopefully they are still a thing by then! Until next time, hwyl fawr!.

03/03/2021

Hello everyone (or anyone), so today I was not motivated to work at all,from the moment I woke up I felt tired and my head just not in it. So intead of reading, I decided to exttend on of my repositories MOBO! So I thought in the this blog I'll talk a little about what MOBO is. MOBO stands for MOdular Bayesian Optimization, and when I started implementing this, it was for two reasons, one, a model for me to use easily when I came to carrying out experiments for Bayesian Optimization and two, a repository that was clear for people to debug if needed. I'm not going to lie, this implementation is definetly not the best out there, like there are actual company / univeristy funded libraries you can use like GPyTorch (facebook) and GPy (Sheffield Univeristy) what I've found whilst trying to find out how they use it is their implementation is complicated! And probably just puts people off trying to understand the process and how to program it! The changes I am planning to implement is the Heteroptopic LCM Multi-Task Gaussian Process, first proposed in 2008 the Multi-Task Gaussian Process leverages similarities between tasks through a covariance function matrix. This matrix is learned through optimizing the log-likelihood. So for now, this is my next target....I might implement an moduler Evolutionary Algorithm Library! Until next time, hwyl fawr!.

Amazon Kindle Publishing and Large-Scale Evolutionary Optimization

25/02/2021

Hi everyone (well im assuming atleast one person will read this), so as a person I'm always thinking of ways I can make passive income (and to date I have no methods), but I found out that your can upload books to Amazon Kindle for free! (well Amazon get some royalities) So in short, I come from quite a creative family, my mothers a musician, dads and full-time artist my sister is like a wizzz on the Harp and Piano and plays for the UK National Youth Orchestra (link here). One of the things I think has gone un-noticed during this Pandemic, or even something that has been given not much attention is the affect this has had on children with learning difficulties, especially those who are young and dont understand whats going on. Soo, with all this creativity in my family I thought, what if we tried to write a short (very short) "childrens" book as stories that can maybe better explain to these young people, and even stories that could help them with growing up. Anyway that was my business idea for the day aha!
Secondly, Large-Scale Evolutionary Optimization, is the optimization of high-dimensional problems. So a method that is mostly used in the literature split the large objective vector into smaller discrete groups and try to optimize each "chunk" of the large-vector on its own. My thought is, what is different dimensions of the problem share similar characteristics? That is, it may be really large problem, but maybe the shape of different dimensions are the same or even have similar optimums for the problem? I wonder if anyone has thought of applying a Multi-Task Evolutionary Algorithm to the decomposed problem instead of the Coevolutionary method that most decomposing algorithms use? Anyway, thats my two ideas for today, until next time, Hwyl Fawr!
